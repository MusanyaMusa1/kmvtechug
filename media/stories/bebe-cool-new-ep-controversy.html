Bebe Cool Drops New EP Amid Fresh Controversies and Social Media Buzz

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 10 July 2026 · English
📷 Headphones and a mixing desk in a music studio

Veteran Ugandan musician Bebe Cool commanded headlines once again this week as he released a new EP while navigating public controversies, including the deletion of certain posts and renewed talk about past business deals.

The self-proclaimed "Big Talent" dropped the project to mixed but passionate reactions from fans. The EP showcases his signature blend of reggae, dancehall, and contemporary sounds, with tracks touching on personal growth, industry politics, and celebration; early reviews praise the production quality and his enduring vocal delivery.

In a statement accompanying the release, Bebe Cool acknowledged deleting some older posts but framed it as part of moving forward and focusing on positive energy. Supporters defend the move as an artiste's right to curate his online space, while critics see it as avoiding accountability on certain past matters.

A close associate in the industry told King Musah Media: "Bebe has always been outspoken. Whether you agree with him or not, he stays relevant. This new body of work shows he is still creating and not resting on past glory." Several tracks were already climbing local charts, with promoters hinting at possible live performances to support the project.
