President Museveni Sacks Six Engineers Over Delays in Busega-Mpigi Expressway

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 20 July 2026 · English
📷 A highway construction project in progress

In a decisive move to address persistent delays in key infrastructure projects, President Yoweri Kaguta Museveni has sacked six engineers from the Ministry of Works and Transport. The dismissals are linked to the slow progress on the Busega-Mpigi Expressway.

Works and Transport Minister Hon. Fred Byamukama confirmed the development while appearing before Parliament's Committee on Physical Infrastructure. He revealed that he received the dismissal letter from the President earlier that morning. The six engineers had accompanied the Minister to the parliamentary session.

The Busega-Mpigi Expressway, a critical component of Uganda's road infrastructure expansion, has faced repeated delays, raising concerns among the public and stakeholders about project timelines, costs, and overall efficiency in the sector.

This action underscores the government's commitment to accountability and performance in the delivery of flagship projects. However, some voices, including members of the public and parliamentarians, have called for broader investigations into the Ministry of Works and Transport to address systemic issues that may have contributed to the delays.

It is important to note that while swift personnel changes signal strong leadership intent, sustainable improvements will require transparent probes, stronger project oversight, and addressing underlying challenges such as procurement processes, funding, and technical capacity. King Musah Media will continue following this story, including any further parliamentary discussions and updates on the Busega-Mpigi Expressway.
