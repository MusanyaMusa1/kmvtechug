Educational Trip Ends in Tragedy: 20 Pupils and School Director Killed in Kapchorwa Bus Crash

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 17 July 2026 · English
📷 Emergency responders at the scene of a road accident in Kapchorwa

A school bus ferrying pupils from King David Junior School Ndejje overturned in a tragic accident in Kapchorwa District on Thursday evening, leaving at least 20 pupils and one adult dead.

The bus, registration number UA 108BQ, was returning from an educational tour to the popular Sipi Falls when the accident occurred around 8:00 p.m. at Chekwatit village, Kimawa Parish, Kawowo Sub-county. According to officials, the vehicle developed mechanical problems before the crash.

Kapchorwa Resident District Commissioner Bayole Stanley initially confirmed 19 deaths (18 pupils and one adult). However, updated reports from police and other sources indicate 20 pupils and the school's founder and director, Mr. Tadeo Ssekade, perished in the incident, bringing the confirmed death toll to 21. Several other pupils and crew members sustained injuries and were rushed to Kapchorwa General Hospital for treatment.

Warnings ignored? Area leaders and witnesses allege that locals had warned the driver about the bus's faulty condition earlier in the journey, but the trip continued. Twalla Fadil, General Secretary of the Patriotic League of Uganda (PLU) and area MP, expressed deep sorrow, stating the accident "could have been avoided" if the warnings had been heeded. Uganda Police Force spokesperson Michael Kananura confirmed the fatal crash and noted that rescue operations were conducted at the scene, with investigations into the exact cause, including the reported mechanical failure and possible driver decisions, ongoing.

Heartbreak for families and the nation. The tragedy has sent shockwaves across the country, with parents and guardians rushing to Kapchorwa to identify their children. Videos circulating on social media show distraught teachers and responders at the scene, highlighting the immense grief felt by the school community. King David Junior School Ndejje, located in Makindye Ssabagabo Municipality, Wakiso District, had organised the trip as part of its educational programme, and many Ugandans are now questioning the safety protocols for long-distance school tours, especially for young primary pupils.

This incident comes shortly after other school-related road accidents, raising fresh concerns about vehicle maintenance, driver fatigue, and oversight of educational excursions. Authorities have urged calm as identification and support for affected families continue, with the Uganda Police and relevant ministries expected to release further details, including the full list of victims, once next of kin are notified.

King Musah Media extends its deepest condolences to the families of the deceased, the staff and pupils of King David Junior School Ndejje, and the entire nation. May the souls of the departed rest in eternal peace.

This story is developing. King Musah Media will provide updates as more information becomes available from official sources.
