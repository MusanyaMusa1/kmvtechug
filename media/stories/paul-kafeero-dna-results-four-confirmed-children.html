DNA Results Out: Only Four Confirmed as Biological Children of Kadongo Kamu Icon Paul Kafeero

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 25 June 2026 · English
📷 A portrait tribute display for the late musician Paul Job Kafeero

For nearly two decades, the name Paul Job Kafeero has evoked not only the golden voice of Kadongo Kamu but also a painful shadow of family discord. Since the celebrated musician's death in 2007, disputes over his vast estate and the true identity of his biological children have played out publicly, often overshadowing his immense musical legacy.

The latest chapter began gaining fresh momentum this year when Minister of State for Local Government, Hon. Balaam Barugahara Atenyi — a longtime friend and promoter of the late singer — stepped in to broker a definitive resolution. On June 1, 2026, under a court order, Kafeero's remains were exhumed in Nkokonjeru, Mukono District, to allow for DNA sampling, conducted with strict forensic protocols involving the Uganda Police Forensic Directorate and the Government Analytical Laboratory.

Tension filled the air at the Uganda Police Headquarters in Naguru on June 25 as family members, claimants, clan leaders, and journalists gathered for the long-awaited announcement. Officials confirmed that only four out of 25 individuals who underwent testing are biologically related to Kafeero: Simon Peter Kafeero, Elizabeth Nagawa, Thomas Kafeero (also known as Swazi or Schwarzenegger), and Benedicto Kafeero (also known as Dube). The results, derived from samples taken from Kafeero's remains, matched across independent laboratories.

Director of Forensics Andrew Mubiru emphasized the rigor of the process: "Science does not lie. These findings provide a clear legal and factual framework." Minister Balaam, visibly emotional yet composed, called on the confirmed children to rise to the responsibility of preserving their father's legacy and estate, which includes properties in areas like Kajjansi, Kyaggwe, and Buikwe.

What next? Deputy Speaker of Parliament Thomas Tayebwa publicly expressed surprise at some outcomes, citing strong physical resemblances, and suggested broader family testing; Minister Balaam responded by urging respect for science while referencing Kafeero's own songs as sources of wisdom, and hinted at possible overseas verification if needed. For the 21 unconfirmed claimants, legal experts suggest the results will carry significant weight in any remaining court matters, though mediation and counselling have been proposed to help all parties find peace.

A senior musician who knew Paul intimately told King Musah Media: "We laughed, we cried, and we made music together. Paul's spirit was bigger than any dispute. The confirmed children should now focus on honouring him through his art — perhaps by ensuring his unreleased works see the light of day and supporting upcoming artists in the Kadongo Kamu tradition." As one friend put it: "The Golden Boy's voice will outlive all of us. Let that be the true inheritance."
