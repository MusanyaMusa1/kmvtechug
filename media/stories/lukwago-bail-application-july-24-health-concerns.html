High Court to Hear Erias Lukwago Bail Application on July 24 Amid Health Concerns and Treason-Related Charges

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 21 July 2026 · English
📷 The exterior of a hospital building

The High Court in Kampala has scheduled July 24, 2026, to hear the bail application of former Kampala Lord Mayor and senior lawyer Erias Lukwago, who faces charges of misprision of treason. The case revolves around allegations that between 2021 and November 2024, Lukwago had knowledge of an alleged plot involving his clients — including Dr. Kizza Besigye and Hajji Obeid Lutale Kamulegeya — to overthrow the government but failed to report it or take preventive steps.

Lukwago, a vocal opposition figure and president of the People's Front for Freedom, denies the charges. As Besigye's lawyer, he has been central to high-profile political cases, adding layers of scrutiny to his own prosecution. His situation intersects with the re-arrest of figures like Muwanga Kivumbi, reflecting broader security operations targeting perceived threats.

Health deterioration and Mulago report. Lukwago's remand has been marked by serious health complications. Following his arrest on June 15, 2026, reports emerged of a hypertension crisis (readings reportedly reaching 217/100) and aggravated spinal issues. He was transferred to Mulago National Referral Hospital after a court order, where a panel of senior consultants assessed him. The Mulago medical report confirms multiple chronic conditions: well-controlled hypertension, unresolved post-COVID-19 lung disease with mild bronchiectasis, persistent tinnitus and loss of smell, residual cervical radiculitis post-spinal surgery in India, and non-erosive gastroesophageal reflux disease with hiatus hernia.

Doctors recommended continued medication, a well-ventilated environment, ongoing ear treatment, and potential further evaluation by his Indian spine surgeons. Lukwago has remained frail, in pain, and under treatment, with his legal team describing him as weak while praising Mulago's care within its capacity; plans for possible transfer abroad for specialised care have been discussed.

Bold questions on procedure. Lukwago's arrest and initial detention sparked controversy over the manner of handling a senior advocate and former elected leader, which his lawyers say contributed directly to his medical distress. The charge of misprision of treason — carrying potential life imprisonment — has fuelled claims of political targeting. Prosecutors maintain the case involves national security and ongoing investigations, justifying remand; courts have so far denied bail, citing flight risk, public influence, and the gravity of the offence, while ordering medical care.

As the July 24 hearing approaches, the judiciary faces an opportunity to demonstrate impartiality. The public will watch closely whether medical realities influence the outcome and if the case proceeds on evidence rather than political optics. King Musah Media will report developments factually as they unfold.
