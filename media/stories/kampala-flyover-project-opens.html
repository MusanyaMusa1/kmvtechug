Kampala's New Flyover Finally Opens to Traffic

MK
Musah Kironde
Published 15 July 2026 · English
📷 Traffic flowing under the newly opened Kampala flyover at dusk

Motorists in Kampala woke up to shorter commutes on Tuesday as the long-awaited Clock Tower flyover officially opened to traffic, three years after construction began.

The Uganda National Roads Authority says the interchange is expected to ease congestion at one of the city's busiest junctions, where commuters have endured detours and gridlock since 2023.

"This is a project that has tested the patience of this city, but the outcome speaks for itself," said a UNRA spokesperson at the opening ceremony.

Business owners along the affected route say they are hopeful the improved flow of traffic will bring back customers who avoided the area during construction.

King Musah Media will continue to follow how the new interchange affects commute times across the city in the coming weeks.
