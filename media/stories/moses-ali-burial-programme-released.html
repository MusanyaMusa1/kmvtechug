Tentative Burial Programme Released for Uganda's Veteran Statesman Gen. Hon. Moses Ali

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 19 July 2026 · English
📷 A mosque with worshippers gathered for prayer

The family of the late Gen. Hon. Moses Ali, in collaboration with the Government of Uganda, has released a tentative burial programme for the veteran politician, military officer, and former Second Deputy Prime Minister, who passed away on Saturday, July 18, 2026, at the age of 87.

Gen. Ali, a towering figure in Uganda's political and military history, died at Nakasero Hospital in Kampala after a period of illness. President Yoweri Museveni described him as a dedicated servant of the nation whose contributions, particularly in West Nile as a statesman and modern farmer, will be greatly missed.

Sunday, July 20, 2026: 10:00 AM — Janaza prayer at Old Kampala Mosque. 12:00 PM — Janaza prayer at Kibuli Mosque. 4:00 PM — Janaza prayer at the mosque in Naguru, after which the body will be taken to his residence in Naguru for prayers and an overnight vigil.

Monday, July 21, 2026: 10:00 AM — The body will be taken to Parliament for an official session and to lie in state. 1:00 PM — Ceremony at Kololo Airstrip; following the Presidential address, the body will be airlifted to Yumbe for Janaza prayers. By 6:00 PM — the body will be airlifted from Yumbe to Adjumani, where it will remain overnight.

Wednesday, July 23, 2026: Morning — The body will be taken to Paridi Stadium for a special gathering involving the 15 districts of West Nile. 3:00 PM — Janaza prayer at Abirichaku. 4:00 PM — Burial.

The programme reflects the high esteem in which Gen. Ali was held, combining religious rites, state honours, and a final homecoming to his roots in Adjumani District. Born on April 5, 1939, in Atabo Parish, Adjumani District, Gen. Ali was a lawyer by training and enjoyed a long, distinguished career spanning military service, politics, and public administration, remaining active until his final days as MP for Adjumani West County.

Tributes continue to pour in from across the political divide, with many describing the late Gen. Ali as a patriot, freedom fighter, and bridge-builder, especially in the West Nile region. King Musah Media joins the nation in mourning this great loss and extends heartfelt condolences to the family, friends, and the people of Adjumani and Uganda at large. May his soul rest in eternal peace.
