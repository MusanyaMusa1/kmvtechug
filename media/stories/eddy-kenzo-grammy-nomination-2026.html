Mixed Emotions as Eddy Kenzo Earns Grammy Nomination but Misses the Ceremony

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 15 February 2026 · English
📷 A golden gramophone-style award trophy

Ugandan music star Eddy Kenzo received a proud moment for the country this awards season after securing a nomination in the Best African Music Performance category at the 2026 Grammy Awards, yet the artiste did not attend the event, triggering a wave of mixed reactions.

The nomination sparked national celebration, with many viewing it as recognition of Kenzo's global impact and consistent work in promoting Ugandan and African sounds internationally. Social media filled with congratulatory messages from fans, fellow musicians, and public figures.

News that Kenzo would miss the ceremony due to urgent personal reasons dampened some of the excitement. In a message to fans, the artiste expressed gratitude for the nomination while explaining the circumstances that prevented his presence in Los Angeles.

One prominent Ugandan musician commented: "A Grammy nod is huge for Eddy and for all of us. Missing the event is unfortunate, but the nomination itself opens doors. We celebrate the win for Uganda." The development fuelled broader conversation about the challenges African artistes face balancing international recognition with personal and logistical realities.
