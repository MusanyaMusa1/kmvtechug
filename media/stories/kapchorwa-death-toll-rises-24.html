UPDATED: Death Toll in King David Junior School Bus Crash Rises to 24 as Three More Pupils Succumb to Injuries

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 18 July 2026 · English
📷 Police officers at the scene of the Kapchorwa road accident investigation

The death toll from the tragic King David Junior School bus crash in Kapchorwa has risen to 24 after three more pupils died while receiving treatment, according to the latest police update.

Traffic Director AIGP Lawrence Nuwabiine confirmed the increase on Saturday. The victims now include 23 pupils and one adult. The crash happened on the evening of Thursday, July 16, 2026, at Chekwatit Village, Kimawa Parish, Kawowo sub-county, as the bus returned from an educational tour to Sipi Falls.

Preliminary findings show the driver lost control of the Isuzu bus (registration UA 108BQ), causing it to veer off the road, strike a large roadside rock, and overturn. Investigations into the exact cause, including possible mechanical failure and overloading concerns, remain ongoing. Several other pupils and adults were injured and hospitalized.

Heartbreaking scenes at school. Parents continue to gather at King David Junior School in Ndejje amid the unfolding tragedy. The incident has left the school community and the nation in mourning.

In response, the government has suspended all school trips and educational excursions nationwide. Acting Education Minister Dr. John Chrysestom Muyingo described the move as an immediate precautionary measure pending a full review of safety guidelines and vehicle standards for student transport. Reports also indicate the school itself may face temporary closure.

This is one of the deadliest road accidents involving Ugandan school children in recent years and comes shortly after other school bus incidents, intensifying calls for stricter enforcement of road safety rules, better vehicle maintenance, and limits on nighttime travel for learners.

Police have not yet released the full list of victims' identities. Families are being supported through dedicated coordination desks at the school and hospitals.

King Musah Media extends condolences to the bereaved families, the injured children, school staff, and the entire nation. We will provide further updates as more details, including official statements and victim identifications, emerge. For support or inquiries, contact authorities at King David Junior School or the relevant health facilities in Kapchorwa and Mbale.
