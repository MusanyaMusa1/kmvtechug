Court Gives Security Agencies 3 Days to Account for Missing Muwanga Kivumbi Amid Mounting Pressure and Rumours

MM
Musanya Musa
Published 23 July 2026 · English
📷 The exterior of the High Court of Uganda

In a significant judicial intervention, High Court Justice Collins Acellam has ordered the Chief of Defence Forces and the Inspector General of Police to report within three days on the whereabouts and circumstances surrounding the continued detention of Hon. Muhammad Muwanga Kivumbi. The directive comes as speculation intensifies over the NUP leader's fate more than ten days after his abduction.

This development marks a critical escalation in efforts to pierce the veil of silence that has enveloped Kivumbi since July 10, underscoring growing institutional pushback against opaque security operations while highlighting the limits of executive impunity in the face of judicial scrutiny.

Judicial demand for answers. Justice Acellam's order requires security chiefs to clarify Kivumbi's location, the agency holding him, and the legal basis for his detention. The move responds to persistent concerns raised in habeas corpus applications and parliamentary queries. Kivumbi's lawyers have long argued that his incommunicado status violates constitutional protections, including the right to be produced in court within 48 hours.

The ruling arrives against a backdrop of unconfirmed but persistent rumours — amplified on social media and in opposition circles — alleging torture, severe injury, or even death. While no credible evidence has substantiated the most extreme claims, the absence of official information has understandably fuelled public anxiety and distrust.

The abduction and its aftermath. Kivumbi was granted bail by the International Crimes Division on July 9 after 169 days on remand over terrorism charges linked to Butambala post-election violence. Hours later, on July 10, armed operatives — including men in uniform — intercepted him at a Mpigi roadblock in a coordinated operation involving police and plainclothes personnel. He was bundled into an unmarked vehicle and has not been publicly seen or heard from since. Family-organised prayers in Butambala drew senior opposition figures, and NUP leader Bobi Wine has publicly cited reports of torture at a military facility and demanded accountability.

The imperative of transparency. Investigations into serious allegations like terrorism require diligence. However, when court bail is swiftly followed by extra-judicial action and prolonged secrecy, it invites accusations of lawfare and abuse of power. The three-day clock is ticking, and Ugandans deserve closure on whether Kivumbi is alive and well, facing legitimate charges through due process, or has once again seen the state prioritise control over constitutional norms. Justice Acellam's order is a welcome assertion of judicial authority; the nation awaits the response.
