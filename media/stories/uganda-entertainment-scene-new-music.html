Uganda's Entertainment Scene Lights Up with New Music Releases and Live Concerts

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 14 July 2026 · English
📷 Concert crowd with stage lighting at a Kampala venue

Uganda's vibrant entertainment industry continues to thrive as local artists release fresh tracks and stage memorable live performances, captivating audiences both at home and across the region. Singers like Guvnor Ace have dropped new Afrobeat bangers, signalling a strong comeback and renewed creative energy in the music scene.

The debut concert by Ashaba Music at a Kampala venue drew large crowds, showcasing the growing appeal of live events as the economy opens up. Beauty pageants such as the crowning of Janet Nabukeera as Miss Tourism Bunyoro 2026/2027 have added glamour and promoted cultural tourism.

Industry players attribute the surge to improved digital platforms and increasing youth engagement, and with the new financial year budget emphasising service sector growth, stakeholders hope for more government support to position Ugandan talent on the global stage — an industry that provides employment while serving as a tool for cultural diplomacy and national branding.
