No Namboole, No AFCON: PM Nabanja Issues Stern Warning as Stadium Delays Threaten 2027 Dreams

MM
Musanya Musa
Published 01 July 2026 · English
📷 Mandela National Stadium Namboole exterior

Prime Minister Robinah Nabanja has drawn a firm line in the sand: without the timely renovation of Mandela National Stadium Namboole, there will be no AFCON for Uganda. Addressing senior government officials and technical teams in a high-level meeting, the Prime Minister demanded immediate answers and accelerated action on the long-delayed upgrades to the country's iconic venue, as Uganda prepares to co-host the 2027 Africa Cup of Nations alongside Kenya and Tanzania.

Namboole is more than a stadium — it is the heartbeat of Ugandan football, the stage where Cranes legends have been born. The UPDF Engineering Brigade, tasked with the second phase of renovations estimated at around $60 million, has been under pressure to deliver by December 2026, but CAF inspectors have previously flagged timelines, and the scale of the upgrade — expanded seating, modern facilities, a new hotel component, and continental-standard compliance — remains a heavy lift.

Prime Minister Nabanja's intervention comes at a pivotal time, with AFCON 2027 just months from the final preparatory window; any further slippage could force difficult conversations with CAF and potentially compromise Uganda's hosting rights. Sports Minister Peter Ogwang and other stakeholders have repeatedly expressed confidence in the Engineering Brigade's ability to deliver, but what's needed now, officials say, is execution: clear milestones, transparent progress reporting, and accountability at every level.

For Ugandan football fans, the stakes are personal. Hosting AFCON would be the ultimate reward — an opportunity to showcase talent, boost tourism, create jobs, and inspire the next generation. The renovations at Namboole, and supporting infrastructure at venues like Hoima, will need to be fast-tracked without compromise if Uganda is to be ready when the continent arrives in 2027.
