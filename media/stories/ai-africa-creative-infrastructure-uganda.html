AI Emerging as Africa's Next Creative Infrastructure, Uganda Included

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 17 July 2026 · English
📷 Person working on a laptop with data visualisations

Artificial Intelligence is rapidly becoming a cornerstone of creative and economic development across Africa, with Uganda actively exploring its potential in innovation and digital transformation. Experts highlight how AI tools are supporting content creation, agriculture analytics, healthcare diagnostics, and small business operations in the country.

As part of broader technology adoption efforts, Ugandan entrepreneurs and institutions are integrating AI to boost efficiency and competitiveness, aligning with national goals for digital economy growth under the current budget framework. Challenges such as skills gaps and infrastructure remain, prompting calls for increased investment in education and training.

With initiatives like enhanced ICT policies from the Ministry of ICT and National Guidance, the country aims to harness technology for job creation and sustainable development in the coming years.
