Parliament Opens Debate on New Digital Services Tax Bill

GN
Grace Namutebi
Published 14 July 2026 · English
📷 Members of the Uganda Parliament seated in the chamber during a session

The Ugandan Parliament began debate this week on a bill that would introduce a levy on revenue generated by digital service providers operating in the country, including streaming and e-commerce platforms.

Supporters of the bill argue it will widen the tax base at a time when digital commerce is growing quickly, while opponents say the cost is likely to be passed on to consumers.

The Ministry of Finance says the bill is part of a broader push to modernise Uganda's tax system in line with regional partners who have introduced similar measures.

Civil society groups have asked for public hearings before the bill proceeds to a second reading, saying the current draft was tabled with limited consultation.

The committee is expected to report back to the House within three weeks.
