Minister Orders Closure of Namisindwa Secondary School Over Poor Sanitation and Alleged Fund Mismanagement

MM
Musanya Musa
Published 16 July 2026 · English
📷 Dilapidated school toilet block made of rusted iron sheets

Local Government Minister Balaam Barugahara has directed the immediate temporary closure of Namisindwa Secondary School following a surprise inspection that revealed shocking lapses in sanitation and student welfare.

During the on-site visit, the minister expressed dismay at the inadequate toilet facilities serving more than 550 students. Eyewitness footage shows the minister pointing out dilapidated structures pieced together from rusted corrugated iron and wooden panels, describing the conditions as unacceptable for a learning environment.

In addition to the sanitation crisis, Barugahara ordered a full investigation into allegations of mismanagement, including the possible misuse of government funds and inflated pupil enrollment figures. Reports from the inspection also indicate that the headteacher has been ordered arrested pending further inquiries.

"Schools must prioritize the health and safety of our children," the minister emphasized in video circulating on social media. The move comes amid growing public concern over standards in rural public institutions.

Local residents and education stakeholders have welcomed the decisive action, though some are calling for similar inspections across the district and beyond. The school will remain closed until minimum standards are met, with education authorities tasked to facilitate the transfer or alternative arrangements for affected students.

The incident highlights persistent challenges in Uganda's education sector, where infrastructure deficits and accountability issues continue to affect learning outcomes, particularly in under-resourced districts. The Ministry of Local Government has promised a comprehensive report on the findings in the coming days.
