Makerere University Embraces Digital Era with E-Certificates and E-Transcripts

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 21 July 2026 · English
📷 A university graduation ceremony with graduates in gowns

Makerere University, Uganda's oldest and most prestigious institution of higher learning, has taken a bold step into the digital age by introducing electronic transcripts and certificates for its graduates. The move, announced by Academic Registrar Prof. Mukadasi Buyinza, is set to transform how academic credentials are accessed, verified, and shared, sparing thousands of alumni the perennial hassle of returning to campus for certified hard copies.

"The new e-transcripts and e-certificates will enable graduates to access their academic documents electronically while allowing employers and other third parties to verify their authenticity online with the graduate's permission," Prof. Buyinza explained.

For decades, Makerere graduates — many of whom pursue opportunities abroad or in the private sector — have endured delays, travel costs, and bureaucratic bottlenecks to obtain official documents. The e-system promises to eliminate much of that friction, aligning with global trends in higher education where secure, instantly verifiable digital credentials are becoming standard.

Success will hinge on robust cybersecurity to protect sensitive student data, user-friendly platforms accessible even to graduates with limited internet, and reliable backup systems. The university must also address equity concerns, ensuring rural or less tech-savvy alumni are not left behind during the transition.

For Uganda's youthful population, where higher education is a key ladder out of poverty, faster credentialing means quicker entry into the job market and stronger linkages between academia and industry. If successful, Makerere's model could inspire other Ugandan universities to follow suit, accelerating the country's digital transformation in education.
