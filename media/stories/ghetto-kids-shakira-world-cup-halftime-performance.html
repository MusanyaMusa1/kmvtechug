BREAKING: Uganda's Ghetto Kids Shine on the World Stage Alongside Shakira at 2026 FIFA World Cup Final

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 19 July 2026 · English
📷 Young Ugandan dancers performing on a large stadium stage

In a moment of pure pride for Uganda and Africa, the world-famous Ghetto Kids (Triplets Ghetto Kids) danced their way into history at the 2026 FIFA World Cup final halftime show. The talented young performers from Kampala took center stage alongside global superstar Shakira and Nigerian artist Burna Boy, waving the Ugandan flag with infectious energy before a global audience of millions.

The group joined Shakira during the performance of the official World Cup anthem "Dai Dai." Barefoot and dressed in vibrant, colorful attire matching the show's festive theme, the Ghetto Kids brought raw talent, joy, and cultural representation from the streets of Uganda to the New York New Jersey Stadium.

From the streets to the spotlight. Founded as part of the Triplets Ghetto Kids organization, the troupe uses music, dance, and drama to support disadvantaged, orphaned, and street children in Uganda. What began as a grassroots initiative has grown into an international sensation, with their viral videos capturing hearts worldwide — Shakira personally invited them after discovering their passionate dance videos to "Dai Dai," turning their dream into reality.

One young performer captured the emotion perfectly: "From the streets, from ghettos, we are on this stage like it's possible." Their appearance was more than entertainment — it was a powerful statement of resilience, hope, and the transformative power of the arts for children facing hardship. The Ghetto Kids shared the stage during the first-ever World Cup final halftime show, a star-studded spectacle that also featured Madonna, BTS, Justin Bieber, and others.

A proud moment for Uganda. As Uganda celebrated from home and across the diaspora, the Ghetto Kids' performance symbolized national pride on football's grandest platform. Social media erupted with congratulations, with many Ugandans hailing the group as true ambassadors for the country. While Spain ultimately triumphed on the pitch with a 1-0 extra-time victory over Argentina, the Ghetto Kids' story stole hearts off it.

Uganda, you shone brightly. The Ghetto Kids are not just performers — they are proof that with talent, determination, and opportunity, anything is possible. Their moment with Shakira will be remembered as one of the most inspiring highlights of the 2026 World Cup.
