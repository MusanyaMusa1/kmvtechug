BREAKING: Spain Crowned World Champions Once More – Torres' Extra-Time Strike Ends Argentina's Reign

KM
King Musah Media Sports Desk
Published 19 July 2026 · English
📷 Spain players celebrating with the World Cup trophy

In a tense, hard-fought final worthy of the occasion, Spain defeated defending champions Argentina 1-0 in extra time to claim the 2026 FIFA World Cup title at the New York New Jersey Stadium. Ferran Torres' clinical finish in the 106th minute delivered La Roja their second World Cup crown, 16 years after Andrés Iniesta's iconic strike in 2010.

The match, played before a raucous crowd of more than 80,000 that included U.S. President Donald Trump and FIFA President Gianni Infantino, showcased Spain's trademark possession dominance against a resilient but ultimately toothless Argentine side. Spain controlled proceedings for long stretches, outshooting Argentina 20-2 and enjoying the lion's share of possession, yet found themselves repeatedly thwarted by the heroics of goalkeeper Emiliano Martínez.

The breakthrough finally arrived six minutes into the second period of extra time. Pedro Porro delivered a dangerous cross from the right, Nico Williams rose at the back post to nod the ball back across goal, and Torres — introduced as a substitute — arrived unmarked to smash a half-volley into the roof of the net. The goal echoed Iniesta's decisive strike in Johannesburg and proved decisive.

Moments before extra time, Argentina were reduced to 10 men when midfielder Enzo Fernández received a second yellow card for a reckless challenge on Pau Cubarsí. The numerical disadvantage proved insurmountable as Spain maintained relentless pressure while Argentina struggled to create meaningful chances. Lionel Messi, in what may have been his final World Cup appearance, was largely contained by a disciplined Spanish defense that conceded just one goal throughout the entire tournament — a historic defensive record.

Spain captain Rodri, named the tournament's Golden Ball winner, lifted the famous trophy alongside his teammates during an emotional presentation ceremony. At 65, coach Luis de la Fuente became the oldest manager to win a World Cup, capping a remarkable run that also saw Spain become the first nation to hold both the men's and women's World Cup titles simultaneously.

This victory cements Spain's place among football's elite in the modern era. Having already secured the 2024 European Championship, Luis de la Fuente's young, technically gifted squad has now conquered the world with a blend of tactical discipline, attacking flair, and unyielding resilience. For Argentina, the night ended in disappointment — despite a spirited defensive effort and Martínez's record-breaking saves, they could not replicate their 2022 heroics and depart without a third consecutive major title.

As fireworks lit up the New Jersey sky and Spanish fans erupted in celebration, one thing was clear: a new generation of Spanish talents — from Lamine Yamal to Williams, Cubarsí, and Torres — has announced itself on the grandest stage. ¡Campeones del Mundo! Spain, the 2026 FIFA World Cup champions.
