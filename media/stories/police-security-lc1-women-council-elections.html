Police Vow Tight Security for LC1 and Women Council Elections as Uganda Tests Grassroots Democracy

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 21 July 2026 · English
📷 A police officer standing near a polling station

Uganda Police Force has assured the public of robust security arrangements for the upcoming Local Council 1 and Women Council elections, promising a peaceful and credible process at the grassroots level, with elaborate measures in place to safeguard voters, candidates, and polling centres across the country.

These elections represent an important renewal of leadership at the most basic unit of governance, where LC1 chairpersons and women council representatives handle local disputes, mobilisation, and service delivery. In a nation with a history of electoral tensions, the police pledge is timely but will be judged on delivery.

Past LC elections have seen low turnout, logistical hiccups, and isolated violence linked to candidate disputes. Critics often accuse security forces of bias toward ruling party structures, especially in rural areas where opposition voices struggle for space; for these polls to strengthen local democracy, the force must demonstrate impartiality and effective coordination with the Electoral Commission.

Successful, violence-free grassroots voting could rebuild public confidence in the electoral system; any major incidents risk eroding trust further. As voting dates approach, credible elections require political maturity from candidates, vigilance from observers, and an Electoral Commission that delivers logistics without controversy. King Musah Media will monitor developments on the ground.
