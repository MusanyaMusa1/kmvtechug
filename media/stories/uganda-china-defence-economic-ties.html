Uganda Strengthens Defence and Economic Ties with China

KM
King Musah Media News Desk
Published 02 July 2026 · English
📷 Handshake between two officials at a bilateral meeting

Uganda is deepening its strategic partnership with China, particularly in defence cooperation and infrastructure development, as both nations seek to expand mutual economic benefits. Recent engagements have focused on military training, equipment support, and long-term investment in key sectors.

The growing alliance comes as Uganda implements its ambitious national budget aimed at industrialisation and infrastructure expansion. Analysts view the partnership as crucial for technology transfer and capacity building, though it has also sparked discussion on balancing foreign relations with national sovereignty and debt sustainability.

Government officials emphasise that such collaboration will help accelerate projects like road networks and energy initiatives while opening new markets for Ugandan products, reflecting Uganda's active foreign policy approach in an increasingly multipolar world.
