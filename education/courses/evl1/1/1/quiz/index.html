Question 1 of 7
What does Section 2 of the Evidence Act Cap. 8 define evidence to include?
A
Only oral testimony given by witnesses in court
B
Only documents produced before the court
C
All statements the court permits or requires from witnesses, and all documents including electronic records produced for inspection
D
Only physical objects such as weapons or fingerprints
Correct. Section 2 of the Evidence Act Cap. 8 defines evidence broadly to include both oral statements permitted or required by the court and documentary evidence, including electronic records.
Not quite. Section 2 of the Evidence Act Cap. 8 defines evidence broadly. It is not limited to only one category - it covers both oral testimony and documentary evidence including electronic records. The correct answer is C.
Question 2 of 7
Is Evidence Law substantive law or procedural law?
A
Substantive law, because it creates legal rights
B
Procedural law, because it governs how facts are proved in court
C
Neither - it is a separate category of constitutional law
D
Both equally, depending on the case
Correct. Evidence Law is procedural (adjectival) law. It does not create rights or define offences - that is the role of substantive law such as the Penal Code Act Cap. 128. Evidence Law governs the process of proving facts.
Not correct. Evidence Law is procedural law, not substantive law. It does not create rights or define offences. It governs how facts are proved in court. The correct answer is B.
Question 3 of 7
Which of the following is the primary statute governing evidence law in Uganda?
A
The Constitution of Uganda 1995
B
The Magistrates Courts Act Cap. 19
C
The Evidence Act Cap. 8
D
The Trial on Indictments Act Cap. 25
Correct. The Evidence Act Cap. 8 is the primary statute governing evidence in Uganda. The other statutes govern court procedure generally but are not the primary source of evidence rules.
Not correct. The primary statute is the Evidence Act Cap. 8. The Constitution, Magistrates Courts Act, and Trial on Indictments Act are important but they are not the primary source of evidence rules. The correct answer is C.
Question 4 of 7
The Evidence Act Cap. 8 was historically derived from which piece of legislation?
A
The English Criminal Justice Act 2003
B
The Indian Evidence Act of 1872
C
The UK Civil Evidence Act 1995
D
The Roman-Dutch law of evidence
Correct. Uganda's Evidence Act Cap. 8 traces its origins to the Indian Evidence Act of 1872, which was adapted for use across East African colonial territories.
Not correct. Uganda's Evidence Act Cap. 8 was derived from the Indian Evidence Act of 1872. The UK statutes and Roman-Dutch law mentioned in the other options do not apply in Uganda. The correct answer is B.
Question 5 of 7
Which of the following is NOT one of the five core objectives of evidence law discussed in Lesson 1?
A
Search for truth
B
Fair trial principles
C
Maximising the number of witnesses called in every case
D
Protection against wrongful conviction
Correct. The five core objectives are: search for truth, fair trial principles, reliability of evidence, protection against wrongful conviction, and balancing justice with efficiency. Maximising witnesses is not an objective - in fact, evidence law often limits irrelevant evidence to keep proceedings efficient.
Not correct. Look at the five objectives again: truth, fairness, reliability, protection against wrongful conviction, and efficiency. Maximising the number of witnesses is not one of them - it actually contradicts the efficiency objective. The correct answer is C.
Question 6 of 7
What is the relationship between substantive law and evidence law?
A
They are the same thing
B
Substantive law defines rights and obligations, while evidence law governs how those rights and obligations are proved in court
C
Evidence law replaces substantive law in criminal cases
D
Substantive law only applies in civil cases, evidence law only applies in criminal cases
Correct. Substantive law defines what is legally right or wrong (such as the Penal Code Act Cap. 128 defining theft). Evidence law governs how the facts proving or disproving such matters are established in court. Both apply across civil and criminal proceedings.
Not correct. Substantive law and evidence law serve different functions but work together. Substantive law defines rights and offences; evidence law governs how facts related to those rights and offences are proved in court. Both apply in civil and criminal cases. The correct answer is B.
Question 7 of 7
Which constitutional provision guarantees the right to a fair hearing, supporting the fairness objective of evidence law?
A
Article 28 of the Constitution of Uganda 1995
B
Section 30 of the Evidence Act Cap. 8
C
Article 99 of the Constitution of Uganda 1995
D
Section 2 of the Evidence Act Cap. 8
Correct. Article 28 of the Constitution of Uganda 1995 guarantees the right to a fair hearing. This constitutional provision underpins the fairness objective that evidence law gives practical effect to.
Not correct. The constitutional provision guaranteeing the right to a fair hearing is Article 28 of the Constitution of Uganda 1995. Section 30 of the Evidence Act Cap. 8 deals with hearsay, and Section 2 defines evidence. The correct answer is A.
