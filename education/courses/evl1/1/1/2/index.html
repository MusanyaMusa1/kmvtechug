What is Evidence Law?

📹 Video + Text⏱ ~10 min📄 Evidence Act Cap. 8
▶️
What is Evidence Law?Video uploading shortly — read the notes below.

NotebookLM audio discussion · PDF notes downloadable below

📋 Video Summary

This video explains evidence law as the complete body of rules governing how facts are proved or disproved in court. It covers the four fundamental questions evidence law answers in every case, the origin of Uganda's Evidence Act Cap. 8 from the Indian Evidence Act 1872, and the four purposes evidence law serves — finding truth, ensuring fairness, promoting efficiency, and protecting important interests.

Section 1From Evidence to Evidence Law

In Page 1 we established what evidence is. But here is the next question: if the knife, the witnesses, and the letter all exist — how does the court decide which ones to actually look at? How does it decide who can speak, what documents it accepts, how much weight to give each piece?

These decisions are not made randomly. They are governed by rules. The entire body of those rules is what we call Evidence Law.

Section 2Definition of Evidence Law

Evidence Law is the branch of law that governs the means and methods by which facts are proved or disproved in legal proceedings before a court or tribunal. It answers four fundamental questions in every case:

Question 1 — What can be presented? Not everything useful is allowed. Evidence law sets out what material a court will receive and what it will reject.

Question 2 — How must it be presented? There are specific rules about how witnesses testify, how documents are produced, and how electronic records are authenticated.

Question 3 — Who can present it? Evidence law determines who is competent to testify, who can be compelled, and who is protected from compulsion.

Question 4 — What weight does it carry? Once admitted, how much significance can the court attach to it, and does it need support before it can be acted upon?

Section 3Primary Source — Evidence Act Cap. 8
📜 Critical — Know This Cap Number

The primary source of evidence law in Uganda is the Evidence Act Cap. 8, Laws of Uganda. Not Cap. 6. Not Cap. 7. Cap. 8. This is the 2024 revised citation. Use it in all academic and professional work.

The Act was derived from the Indian Evidence Act of 1872, which codified English common law principles for colonial territories. Uganda inherited this framework through the East African legislative tradition and has developed it through decades of Ugandan court decisions.

Section 4Purposes of Evidence Law

Purpose 1 — Finding the truth. Evidence law channels fact-finding so courts work with reliable, tested material rather than rumour or speculation.

Purpose 2 — Fairness to both parties. Both sides can present their case and challenge the other's evidence. This gives effect to Article 28 of the Constitution of Uganda 1995 — the right to a fair hearing.

Purpose 3 — Efficiency of proceedings. Rules of relevancy keep proceedings focused on matters that actually matter. Without them, cases would never end.

Purpose 4 — Protection of important interests. Some information is protected even if relevant — lawyer-client privilege, certain state documents, identities of police informants. Evidence law balances truth against other important values.

Section 5Relationship with Procedure

Evidence law is closely connected to procedural law. Think of it this way: procedural law is the road; evidence law governs the vehicles allowed on that road and the rules of the road itself.

Key procedural statutes alongside the Evidence Act Cap. 8:

📚 Related Statutes

Civil Procedure Act Cap. 282 — governs civil proceedings generally

Trial on Indictments Act Cap. 25 — governs serious criminal trials in the High Court

Magistrates Courts Act Cap. 19 — governs proceedings before magistrates

Constitution of Uganda 1995, Article 28 — guarantees the right to a fair hearing

SummaryKey Points
Key Points — Page 1.1.2
  • Evidence Law is the body of rules governing how facts are proved or disproved in legal proceedings
  • It answers four questions: what can be presented, how, by whom, and with what weight
  • Primary source in Uganda: Evidence Act Cap. 8 — derived from the Indian Evidence Act 1872
  • Four purposes: finding truth, ensuring fairness, promoting efficiency, protecting important interests
  • Evidence law operates alongside the Civil Procedure Act Cap. 282, Trial on Indictments Act Cap. 25, and Magistrates Courts Act Cap. 19
