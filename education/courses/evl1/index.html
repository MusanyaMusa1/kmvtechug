About This Course

Evidence Law 1 covers the complete foundational framework of evidence law as applied in Ugandan courts. Every module is mapped directly to the Evidence Act Cap. 8, Laws of Uganda, and draws on real decisions from the High Court and Supreme Court of Uganda.

The course is designed for university students studying law at Year 2 or Year 3 level who need to understand not just the rules but why they exist and how they apply in real court situations. Content is simplified without sacrificing accuracy you will be able to use what you learn in actual exam and moot settings.

Each lesson combines video explanation, text notes, downloadable PDF materials, and practice quizzes. Every module ends with a graded quiz that counts toward your certificate. The certificate requires an 80% average across all module quizzes.

Course Requirements

  • No prior knowledge of law required this course starts from the very beginning
  • A copy of the Evidence Act Cap. 8, Laws of Uganda is helpful but not required the relevant sections are discussed within each lesson
  • To earn the certificate, you must achieve an 80% average across all 8 module graded quizzes
  • Each module quiz allows a maximum of 3 attempts a 24-hour cooldown applies after 3 failed attempts
  • An active study plan is required to access lesson content, take quizzes, and download PDF notes
  • Registration is free you can view this course preview page without an account

Who This Course Is For

  • Law students at Ugandan universities studying Evidence Law at Year 2 or 3 level
  • Practising advocates who need to refresh or consolidate their knowledge of the Evidence Act Cap. 8
  • Paralegals and legal assistants working in Ugandan courts
  • Anyone preparing for bar examinations or the Law Development Centre diploma
